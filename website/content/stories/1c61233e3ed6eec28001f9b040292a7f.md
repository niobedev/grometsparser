### Part One: The Preparation

The factory was silent at 2 a.m. Allie stood on the metal grating, the hum of idle machinery the only sound. Above her, the dipping vat loomed, a stainless steel behemoth still warm from the day's last batch. The air smelled of cocoa butter and industrial cleaner.

The sign above the loading dock read **Enrobé: Custom Confectionery**. Nothing more. The building was unmarked brick, easy to miss, but the windows glowed warm yellow. Inside, stainless steel counters gleamed next to whimsical displays: a life-sized chocolate carousel horse, a sugar glass chandelier, racks of novelty molds shaped like dinosaurs and ballerinas. Photos lined the far wall, past commissions for weddings, art galleries, and one very elaborate birthday party. This was not a factory. It was an eccentric artist's playground.

She had signed the waivers, chatted with the night guard about his daughter's recital, and told no one where she was going. *Summer would never let her hear the end of this.* Allie smiled at the thought. Some secrets were worth sharing. Some were worth keeping.

A single video camera stood on a tripod near the control panel. Its red light blinked. *Recording.*

She stepped into frame, naked except for the rope web harness already strapped to her body. The nude nylon webbing crossed her chest, her ribs, and her back, converging at a single steel ring between her shoulder blades. Around each of her thighs was a padded leather strap with a small buckle. A matching set was wrapped around each ankle.

Her red hair was pinned up, with two strands left loose to frame her face. In the factory lights, it was a deep, warm auburn. Her bunnygirl ears were already clipped into place, white with pink inside, rising above her head like a crown.

Her heels were already on, black patent leather with a two-inch heel. She had put them on before the harness, before any of it, because being fully coated down to her feet mattered to her. The chocolate would cover everything. The heels would be part of the sculpture.

She thought of David. How he would look at her when she arrived. How his eyes would move over her, slow and wondering. *This is insane*, she told herself. *Absolutely insane.* She smiled anyway.

Then she knelt on the rubber mat before the vat.

First, the eye covers. She pressed a pair of clear plastic shields into place over her eyes, thin and curved, designed to fit over her closed eyelids. Once the chocolate hardened, they would pop out cleanly, leaving her eyes untouched.

Next, the pink ball gag. She placed the small silicone ball behind her teeth and fastened the pink strap around her neck. The ball was smooth and non-stick; chocolate would not cling to it. The pink sat between her lips, bright and visible.

*This is the part he is really going to laugh at*, she thought. *Or really, really appreciate.*

They had joked about something like this once. A late night, just talking, her asking what he would do if she showed up as a chocolate bunny girl. He had laughed. Said he would eat her up. She had not been joking. Not really.

Now here she was.

*This is for him*, she thought. *But also for me.* She paused with the ankle strap in her hand. *Is this too much?* No. She had asked herself that question a hundred times. The answer never changed.

She had done this before, after a fashion. Not the chocolate, but the rest of it, the straps, the helplessness, the feeling of being watched. She had been David's stage assistant for years, the one who got locked in boxes, strapped to boards, suspended in water tanks. The audience always thought she was the unwilling volunteer. But she had never been unwilling. She played the part because the gasps and applause thrilled her, because David's eyes on her from across the stage made her feel like the only person in the room.

*She had spent years on stage, locked in boxes, pretending to be the victim. The audience never knew how much she loved it. David knew. That was different. That was everything.*

This was that feeling turned up to eleven. No audience except the camera. No stage except the factory. But the same trust, the same thrill, the same secret knowledge: she was exactly where she wanted to be, and he would love her for it.

Then the ankles. She pulled each ankle back toward her thigh and secured the strap. The heels made the position more extreme, lifting her feet higher behind her, but she had practiced this at home. Left. Right. Her calves pressed against her hamstrings. She tested the straps and found them solid. She could not straighten her legs, could not stand, and a small thrill ran through her at the thought.

Next, the wrists. She crossed them behind her back and fastened the single locking cuff. The click was loud in the empty factory. She tugged once, then twice. Nothing. Her arms were pinned, her shoulders rolled back, her chest lifted. The helplessness settled over her like a blanket, warm and familiar and welcome. *She remembered the first time he saw her in a harness. The way his breath caught. She wanted that again.*

Finally, the rope web. She shifted her weight, arching slightly, and the steel ring between her shoulder blades rose into easy reach. The hook dangled from above. She leaned back just enough, a small trusting movement, and the hook caught the ring with a click. The cable went taut, pulling her shoulders open and pushing her chest forward. She was completely locked in place. A smile tugged at her lips around the pink ball.

She looked at the camera. *Hi, honey. Surprise.* The plastic covers gave her eyes an eerie glassy sheen, but her expression was unmistakable. She could not smirk with the ball in her mouth, but her eyes did it for her. *This is so extra*, she thought. *Even for me.*

For a moment, she pretended to struggle. A twist of her hips, a tug at her wrists, a futile attempt to straighten her strapped legs. The harness creaked. The cable swayed. A few strands of red hair fell loose across her face.

Then she stopped. She looked at the camera and winked behind the plastic. The red light blinked, recording everything.

### Part Two: The Dipping

The control panel beeped. She had programmed a simple sequence: lift, dip, hold, drain, transfer.

The cable pulled. She rose off the mat, her heels raised behind her. The bunnygirl ears bounced once, then settled. The pink ball gag was bright between her lips.

The arm swung her over the vat and stopped. She hung there, suspended above the brown chocolate surface. The hook creaked softly. The cable swayed, just a little.

Her breath caught. Not from fear. From the weight of what she was about to do.

She let it out slowly. *Ready.*

Like a prize in a claw machine, she thought. Something to be won and claimed and displayed. She felt a little thrill at that, the object of it, wanted and valued and *his*. She winked at the camera again.

Then the arm dropped.

The chocolate was warm, not hot, tempered perfectly to about 90 degrees Fahrenheit (or 32 degrees Celsius, if that is what you know). She had always found it funny that the factory used both systems, a relic of their French-American partnership. She did not question how it worked. It was enough that it worked.

**Her knees broke the surface first.** The warm liquid flowed over her skin like a caress, slow and thick and sweet-smelling. Then her shins, then the backs of her thighs. The chocolate rose, climbing her body from the lowest point upward.

She watched through the clear plastic covers, the brown liquid creeping up her skin. The sensation was intimate, like being touched everywhere at once.

As the chocolate climbed, she felt the last of her mobility disappear. First her legs, then her hips, then her arms. Each inch lost was a small surrender. She did not fight it. She leaned into it. *This is what she wanted. This is what she had been planning for months.*

The chocolate reached her chin. She tilted her head back, nostrils sealed by the small silicone plugs she had inserted minutes ago. The pink ball remained visible, the silicone rejecting the chocolate, leaving it clean and bright.

*What if the plugs fail?* The thought came unbidden. She pushed it away. They had been tested. She had tested them herself. Still, for one breathless second, her heart raced.

*What if the chocolate is too hot? What if the arm stops? What if David doesn't understand?*

She forced herself to breathe. The questions would not stop coming, but she did not need to answer them. She only needed to trust.

Then the arm kept lowering. It covered her mouth, but the pink ball stayed visible, a small bright spot in the brown. Then her nose, her eyes, her forehead, the crown of her head. Her auburn hair disappeared beneath the brown. The ears went last, the white fur darkening as the chocolate swallowed them.

Then she was gone. A smooth brown pool, undisturbed, with only the cable disappearing into its depths.

*Ten seconds*, she had calculated. *Fully submerged.*

She floated in the warmth, weightless, surrounded. This was the point of no return, and she loved it. There was no fear now. There never really had been. Only the slow, certain knowledge that she was becoming something new.

*He had laughed when she first suggested it. She wanted to see that laugh again.*

She was no longer Allie in a factory. She was the gift, the surprise, the chocolate bunny girl who would soon be waiting on David's porch. The gag muffled her smile.

### Part Three: The Lift

The cable pulled her back up. She emerged head first, with chocolate streaming from the plastic eye covers and the silicone plugs in her nostrils. The pink ball was still there, bright and clean.

*It worked*, she thought. *It actually worked.*

A strange calm settled over her. Not the thrill of before, but something quieter. She was no longer becoming. She was made.

Then her shoulders, her chest, her locked wrists, her strapped ankles, and finally her raised heels, the last to leave the liquid, dripping chocolate like twin fountains.

Her ears emerged last, now completely coated. The white fur was brown, the pink inside brown, everything brown. Her auburn hair was hidden beneath the shell. The chocolate had molded itself to her body, to every curve and hollow, clinging like a second skin.

A small robotic arm approached. It plucked the clear covers from her eyes, and with the same motion, pulled the silicone plugs from her nostrils. Both came away cleanly, leaving two perfect oval openings around her eyes and two small holes at her nose. Beneath them, her eyes were untouched, blinking slowly in the factory light. Her nose was clear.

She could breathe freely now, though the pink ball still filled her mouth.

The arm swung her toward the cooling room.

### Part Four: The Cooling

The room was small, refrigerated, and dark. The arm placed her on a metal rack shaped exactly to her kneeling form. Her knees settled into curved depressions. Her raised heels fit into small pockets at the back. The rack had a contoured back support that cradled her spine and left room for her pinned arms.

A soft hum filled the room. She would not suffocate.

The door closed. The temperature dropped. She could not shiver. The chocolate was too thick, too rigid. She could only wait as the cold seeped inward, hardening her from the outside in. The chocolate tightened around her like a gentle embrace turning firm.

*This is not something I can rush*, she realized. *It happens when it happens.*

She stopped waiting. She started being.

She could not move, could not speak, could barely breathe. And yet she had never felt more present, more seen, more *hers*. The helplessness was not a loss. It was a gift she had given herself. Soon, she would give it to David. That thought carried her through the cold.

She watched her reflection in the polished steel, a brown statue kneeling and waiting, pink ball bright between her lips. Beautiful, she thought. David would think so.

*She was not a woman in a factory anymore. She was a surprise on a porch.*

*Ten minutes*, she had calculated. *Then the conveyor.*

The chocolate hardened around her, and she thought of him. His hands. His voice. The way he would look at her when the package arrived.

### Part Five: The Conveyor

The belt started with a low rumble. The cooling room door opened, and the rack rolled forward. The arm lifted her and placed her onto a wide, flat platform conveyor. She remained upright, still kneeling, still locked.

The chocolate was solid now, hard and glossy. The pink ball was a bright accent against the brown.

*Almost there*, she thought. *He has no idea what is coming.*

The platform carried her forward. A robotic arm adjusted her posture slightly. Another scanned her for defects.

She had never felt more ready. Not excited anymore. Not nervous. Just ready.

*Next stop: the box.*

### Part Six: The White Chocolate Accents

She was positioned at another table. White chocolate, warm and creamy, waited in a small vat. A different arm, tipped with a fine nozzle, descended.

It traced a line around her neck, a thick white collar that adhered instantly to the brown shell. It circled each wrist and each ankle, leaving white cuffs gleaming against the chocolate. She imagined David's fingers tracing them later.

It piped a small dollop onto her lower back and pressed a fluffy white chocolate tail into place.

The ears were left alone. The white nozzle traced the inside of each ear. She could not see it, and she could not feel it, but she imagined the white chocolate settling into place, bright against the brown.

White inner ears. Brown outer ears. Chocolate body, white accessories, pink ball gag bright between her lips. She looked like an Easter confection, a present, a prize.

*Finishing touches*, she thought. *Like wrapping a gift.*

She smiled behind the gag.

The platform carried her toward packaging.

### Part Seven: The Packaging

The clear plastic container was waiting, rigid and vacuum formed, the bottom half shaped to fit a kneeling figure. The top half was propped open, hinged at the back.

The arm lifted her and lowered her directly into the bottom half. Perfect fit. Her knees tucked into curved indentations, her folded arms pressed against the small of her back. The back of her head rested in a molded cradle, chin slightly lifted. Her raised heels fit into shaped pockets at the back.

The top half descended with a click. Sealed.

Ventilation holes ran along the top edge, silent and invisible from the front.

She was inside a rigid clear box, still kneeling, arms behind her back. Posed like a collectible, like something precious meant to be unwrapped. But alive. Breathing. Waiting.

*This is it*, she thought. *There is nothing left to do.*

A strange peace settled over her. She had done everything she could. The rest was up to him.

### Part Eight: The Labeling

A printer hummed. A large label was applied to the front of the clear container.

In big bold letters across the top: **FULL SIZED EASTER BUNNY GIRL**

Below that, a pink and white splash, and inside the splash, her name: **Allie**

Below that: *Happy Easter*

And at the bottom: **SHIP TO: David McAllister, 742 Maple Drive**

A second card was attached to the back of the cardboard backing, small and rectangular, designed to look like a standard nutrition label. She had designed it herself.

It read:

**NUTRITION FACTS**

**Serving Size:** 1 whole chocolate bunny girl

**Calories:** 15,000 (from the chocolate; do not eat in one sitting)

**Ingredients:** Milk chocolate, white chocolate accents, silicone ball gag (rubber, not candy), leather straps (not edible), one pair of bunny ears (not edible), one Allie (auburn hair, 5'0", 110 lbs, not totally edible but she does not mind if you nibble).

**Allergens:** Dairy, soy.

**Best By:** Easter Morning

**Warning:** Share with someone you love. 18+ only. Contents are fully consenting and surprisingly comfortable.

No return address. No barcode. Just her name, the holiday, her husband's front door, and a joke only he would fully appreciate.

### Part Nine: The Final Frame

The conveyor started again. She rocked gently inside the clear plastic container as the belt carried her toward the shipping bay. Her arms pressed against the back wall of the box. The white collar and cuffs gleamed under the lights. The pink ball sat bright between her lips.

The camera captured her final moment of visibility. She blinked slowly at the lens, thought of David watching this someday, thought of his hands on her skin.

*She had become exactly what she meant to become.*

She winked.

The bay doors opened. The truck waited. The container was lifted, stacked with others, and sealed inside. Then the dark. The gag muffled her smile. *I am coming home.*

### Part Ten: Easter Morning, The Delivery

David woke to the sound of a truck backing into the driveway. He pulled on a robe and went outside. The driver was lowering a hydraulic lift gate. On it sat a single package, a massive clear plastic container, rigid and sealed, with a cardboard backing.

The container was nearly four feet tall. The clear plastic front showed the figure inside, brown and glossy and kneeling, but the cardboard backing blocked the view from behind.

David stepped closer. The front of the package read **FULL SIZED EASTER BUNNY GIRL**, then her name, then *Happy Easter*, then his address.

His heart did something strange in his chest.

"I think there has been a mistake," he said.

The driver checked his clipboard. "David McAllister?"

"Yes."

"Package is for you. 'Handle with extreme care. Contains perishable art. Do not remove cardboard backing until delivery is complete.'"

The driver wheeled the package onto a dolly, propped it against the front porch, handed David a small envelope, tipped his hat, and drove away.

David stood in his robe, staring at the package. Through the clear plastic, he could see the figure inside. Brown, glossy, kneeling. A pink ball between her lips. White cuffs and collar. Ears, brown outside and white inside.

It looked like a chocolate statue of his wife.

*That is not possible*, he thought.

He stepped closer. The face was hers. The build was hers. The way the head was tilted, just slightly to the left, that was hers too.

*No.*

He ran his hand over the clear plastic. Cold. Smooth. Real.

"Allie?"

Silence. Then movement. A blink. Slow, deliberate.

His heart stopped. "No," he said again, but this time it was not disbelief. It was wonder. She had turned herself into a gift. For him.

Then a new thought hit him, harder than the first. *What if something went wrong?* The ventilation. The cooling. The delivery. She could have been hurt. She could have—

He stopped himself. She was safe. She was here. She had planned this for months. She would not have taken a risk she did not understand.

He pulled the cardboard backing away. He saw her arms pinned behind her back, her wrists locked, the straps around her ankles.

*She trusted him with this. All of it. The planning, the vulnerability, the helplessness. She had made herself completely vulnerable and given him the key.*

He understood. This was not a gift someone had made for her. She had made herself into the gift. For him.

He looked at her, really looked. "You have been planning this for months."

She nodded.

"This is not the stage," he said softly.

She shook her head. Her eyes held his. She did not need words.

He gripped the front seam and pulled. The shell split open. The smell of her, chocolate and skin, hit him immediately. Warm. Sweet. *Her.*

### Part Eleven: The Unwrapping

Allie knelt inside the package, exactly as she had been placed hours ago. Chocolate covered her from the tips of her bunny ears down to the soles of her heels. Only her eyes, her nostril plugs, and the pink ball between her lips remained untouched. Her auburn hair was hidden. Her skin was hidden. Everything that made her a person was hidden beneath the brown shell.

But her eyes were alive. They found his and held. The pink ball was bright between her chocolate-coated lips.

David knelt in front of her. Through the open shell, he could see every curve the chocolate had traced.

"Allie," he said. Just her name. That was enough.

She nodded, her eyes never leaving his.

He traced his finger along her jaw, just below the pink ball. The chocolate was hard and smooth, but beneath it, he could feel the warmth of her skin.

"Allie McAllister," he said softly. "You planned all of this."

Her eyes said *obviously*.

Then he noticed her ears, still attached, still coated, the white fur brown, the pink inside brown. He reached up and gently lifted one ear. The chocolate shell crackled.

"May I?" he asked.

She tilted her head, curious.

He snapped off the tip of the chocolate-coated ear and held it up. "Milk chocolate," he said. "Good choice."

He popped it into his mouth. She laughed, a muffled sound behind the gag, her eyes crinkling.

He kissed her chocolate-coated forehead. "Let's get you out of this."

He lifted her from the package, carried her inside, and set her on the kitchen table. She knelt there, naked beneath the chocolate, bound and gagged, glistening in the morning light.

His hand came up slowly, hesitated, then touched her face, not the chocolate but the small strip of skin just below her eye. His thumb traced her cheekbone.

"Allie," he said again. Different this time. Softer.

She did not look away.

Slowly, he touched the white collar at her throat, tracing the edge where white met brown.

"Let's get you out of this," he said again, and carried her to the bath.

### Part Twelve: The Bath

He carried her to the bathroom. The tub was already filling, warm steam rising from the surface. He tested the temperature and lowered her in.

She knelt in the warm water. The chocolate began to melt in slow rivulets, tracing down her body like dark syrup. Her bare skin appeared beneath in patches, a shoulder, a collarbone, the curve of her breast. Then her auburn hair, first at the temples, then in larger strands, then in a cascade as the chocolate released its grip. It floated around her face like embers on the water.

He unbuckled her ankles from her thighs. His fingers brushed the backs of her knees. She gasped, muffled by the gag. He unlatched the wrist cuff. Her arms fell to her sides.

She flexed her fingers, then let her hands rest on his forearms. Skin to skin for the first time.

He felt her pulse. Fast. Real. *Alive.*

She was no longer the chocolate statue on the porch. She was his wife, and she was here, and she had done this for him.

They stayed like that for a moment. Water running. Chocolate swirling toward the drain. Her hands on his arms. His eyes on hers.

She stood up in the tub. Most of the chocolate was gone, swirling in brown ribbons toward the drain. She kicked off her chocolate-caked heels, and they landed with a soft clatter on the wet tile.

Then he unbuckled the pink ball gag and pulled it gently from her lips.

She worked her jaw, licked chocolate from her lower lip, and looked up at him.

"Hi," David said, his voice low.

"Hi," she said, rough from the gag. "Happy Easter."

He kissed her. She tasted like chocolate and salt and morning. Her hands slid up his arms to his shoulders. Water soaked through his robe. Neither of them cared.

"Let's finish getting you clean," he said.

### Part Thirteen: After

He grabbed a washcloth and knelt beside the tub, running the warm cloth over her shoulders, down her spine, across the backs of her thighs.

He washed the last of the chocolate from her skin, then wrapped a towel around her. She stepped out, and he wrapped another around her hair.

She leaned into him, still warm from the bath, smelling like cocoa and soap. For a moment, neither of them spoke.

Then she said, quietly, "So. How was your Easter present?"

David pulled back just enough to look at her face. Her auburn hair was still wet. Her skin was pink from the warm water. She looked exhausted and pleased with herself.

"You shipped yourself to me in a box," he said.

"I know."

"That is insane."

"I know. But you married me, so whose fault is that really?"

He laughed. "Fair point."

She grinned. "Breakfast?"

"It is Easter. We have chocolate."

She laughed, soft and low. "That chocolate is for you. I am having eggs and hashbrowns. And fruit. And orange juice."

He kissed the top of her head. "Yes, ma'am."

She kissed his cheek. "Now make me breakfast."

He walked to the kitchen. She watched him go, then closed her eyes. It was not just a prank or a present. It was the most honest thing she had ever done. And he understood.

### Epilogue

Later, after breakfast, after she had showered properly and changed into dry clothes, Allie sat on the couch examining her ears. The left one had a clean break at the tip.

"You owe me new ears," she said.

He grinned. "Worth it."

She smiled and leaned her head against his shoulder. Her auburn hair, still damp, smelled like coconut shampoo. The chocolate was gone. The ears were off. The pink ball gag sat on the bathroom counter, rinsed clean.

The video was saved on his laptop.

"So," she said. "What is next?"

He pulled her closer. "Give me two weeks."

She smiled and closed her eyes. This was their language now. She could not wait to learn what he would say next.

*But later*, she thought. *Much later.* For now, she was still here, still warm, still his. That was enough.
