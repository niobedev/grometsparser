“Good, go have fun. I’ll deal with all this.” She gestures at the camper.

“Yea ok.” Ariana disappears inside to put on her swimsuit.

Coming out in her jeans shorts and navy blue swimsuit Kate can’t contain herself and grabs Ariana from behind who squeals in surprise.

“You look so cute.” She whispers in Ariana’s ear. “I wish you could be happy like at home.”

“Then don’t be mean to me…” Ariana retorts with a hushed voice.

Kate looks hurt but doesn’t say anything. She clearly has some ways to go to earn Ariana’s trust again.

“Just love me Kate and don’t be mean or angry, it’s all I ask.” Ariana says, wiggling free from Kate’s hold. “I’ll be at the water.”

Kate looks longingly after her for a moment and continues setting up the camper and is pleased to see that they have a very strong signal on the starlink. She needs to catch up on work.

#### Finding herself

Ariana is glad to be away from the camper for a bit. She sits near the pier wiggling her toes in the water staring at a bottle floating around.

‘I should just run away…’ She contemplates for a moment. ‘Far away, find a better partner.’ She doesn’t want to forgive Kate for the things she said when she was angry. And what’s worse Kate doesn’t even remember she said it.

Another part of her argues for her to make peace with Kate and tough it out. Everyone fights sometimes is the rationalization. It also tells her to not think badly about Kate. She’s the best partner and friend she’s had in forever after-all.

Yet another part of her tells her to stay as she is and quietly follow Kate’s lead from now on. Use the current situation to slip into a completely passive role. This is the part of her that made her want to submit to any partner for as long as she can remember. It’s always lingering under the surface but now it tries to take over her thought process.

The internal struggle drains all of her energy. Ariana hates it and she knows she can’t talk to anyone about it as nobody will understand.

Her thoughts are interrupted by squeaking brakes and the clattering sound of a bicycle being thrown to the ground. Then there is a man’s voice. “Ariana? Oh my… It’s you isn’t it?”

Ariana looks up with sad eyes but doesn’t recognize the guy and ignores him.

“I knew it. It IS you. God damn what a weird coincidence.” The man sounds very cheerful.

“Not interested, I’m married.” Ariana mumbles. “Leave me al…”

She stops mid-sentence. “How do you know my name?” She looks suspicious at the guy.

“I’m Barry, we met in Florida a few years back.” He answers while sitting down next to her. “How are you?”

“I don’t know anyone in Florida…” Ariana rejects him.

“The lifeguard? You sat down near my tower, I treated your neck, remember?” He jogs her mind.

“Oh, right…” Ariana remembers that, but not his face or name.

“Anyway, I always really liked you and tried to find you for ages. Online, in Miami, I asked around everywhere. I even looked at AirBNB and hotel reviews. But I never found any trace of you. And now here you are.”

“Stalker!” Ariana quietly says, “What do you want?”

“Honestly? You…” Barry smiles at her. “Go out with me. Let’s catch up. Come on, don’t say no.”

“Can’t. I’m married.”

“Oh…” Barry’s smile disappears. “But you’re not happy? Or why are you sitting here alone with a face like you just ate a sour grape.”

Ariana vaguely smiles at him, “Just having a fight with my wife…”

“Your wife huh? Where is she? I'll tell her what a dumbo she is for making you sad.” Barry jokingly offers.

“She’ll be angry again if she knows about you.” Ariana sadly says.

“Oh, she’s the jealous type?” Barry concludes, perceptive as always.

Ariana nods.

“Well, then you should tell her yourself, you know? Own that fight. Win it, show her you’re in charge.” Barry suggests.

Not getting a response he then asks. “How is your neck? All better? It’s been what? 2 years?”

“Three, and it’s fine. Look, no scars, just like you said.” Ariana tilts her head and shows him her neck.

Barry scoots closer and looks at it, seeing perfectly smooth brown skin. He resists the strong urge to touch her.

“You’re so cute. You’re sure you’re married? You have no ring…” He flirts with her.

“Barry, please…” Ariana grimaces but leans against him with her back to his arm.

Just like back in Miami, she likes his positive caring energy.

Barry ignores his manners and wraps his arm around her shoulders, so it's loosely around her neck. Ariana tenses up for a moment but doesn’t reject him.

They sit quietly for a few moments.

“Why are you here?” She asks him.

“Hah, I moved here last year. But I don’t like it at all. Miami was much nicer in many ways.” He explains, “Nicer girls too. But seeing you makes it all worth it. It’s meant to be, right?”

“Oh… You’re a lifeguard here too?”

“Yea, well not here, but on a different beach. Past the harbor.” He points to the south. “I give surf lessons too…”

“That’s cool.” Ariana says.

“And you? You live here? I’ve always wondered where you’re from.”

“Toronto…” Ariana says.

“Oh yea, Canada. But really Japan, right?”

“I was born in Japan. I’m from Oita Prefecture.”

“Oh right, cool.” Barry replies. He makes it sound like he knows where that is or what a prefecture is.

“Will you come with me?” Ariana suddenly asks. “Let’s go tell Kate what a dumbo she is.”

Telling a stranger she’s married several times has her realize she needs to stick with Kate.

“Ehhhh…” Barry hesitates, “You’re sure that’s smart?”

“No. But I can’t win a fight alone.” Ariana admits.

Barry ‘hmms’ but doesn’t seem eager to help.

Ariana pushes his arm from her shoulders and gets up. “Can you help me? Please?”

“I don’t even know why you’re fighting. What would I say? And what if she’ll be more upset with you?”

Barry looks for an out, this is not what he envisioned, if nothing else he wants Ariana to throw herself at him and they’d go on dates and fall in love. He has been fantasizing about her for years.

Instead he’s being recruited to resolve a fight between her and her partner.

“Yea, no, but good luck with that.” He refuses. “I’ve been thinking about you often since we first met. When I saw you just now I was so happy. I want to go on a date with you… Not fix your marriage.”

“I see.” Ariana remembers everything now. How he helped her with her neck, and how they chatted like long time friends for a while before he tried to make a move back then too. “But I remember I told you to forget about me.” She smiles innocently at him.

“I can’t OK?” He bursts out, “Everyone I meet I compare to you and nobody is good enough.”

“Barry, please. That’s so stu…” She interrupts herself, “You, ehh, you don’t know me at all. I’ll better go.”

Ariana starts to walk away but he grabs her arm.

“I just think you’re a really good woman to be with and I can’t forget about you. I don’t care if you think that’s stupid.” He blurts out.

Ariana steps closer and hugs him. “Thanks for that. You’re really nice too I’m sure. But you should find another girl. Go to Japan if you like women like me. You’ll find someone.”

Barry nods.

“Start in Oita in Oita if you think I’m pretty. That’s where I’m from. There will be women that are similar to me I’m sure.” She encourages him.

“Oita in Oita?” Barry wonders.

“The city and prefecture have the same name. A prefecture is like a county.” Ariana explains hearing his confusion.

“Ah?” Barry doesn’t sound too happy. “It’s not just about your looks! You’re just so nice and fun. And pretty, for sure…” His voice trails away savoring her touch and smell.

“Anyway, I have to get back to Kate.” Ariana frees herself from his embrace, “Thanks for remembering me, it means a lot.“

Barry dumbly holds her arms and looks down on her.

After a few moments Ariana turns and walks away before they end up kissing again.

“Ariana!” He calls after her. “Tell your Kate she’s a big dumbo, OK? I’ll go to Oita Oita for my next vacation. Thanks!”

“Īdesu yo. Jā ne!” *(Will do. Cya!)* Ariana replies and she disappears behind the pier pillars with a wave. She’s going to hug her dumbo and tell her she loves her.

Arriving at the camper Kate is arguing with 2 cops and a 3rd person.

“Oh there she is. See, I told you I’m not some lonely bum…” Kate fumes. “Hey babe!” She waves at Ariana.

“Hey, what’s up?!” She joins Kate standing on her tip-toes and kisses her on the cheek.

“Well either way,” one of the policemen starts, “This is a parking lot and not a camping. So move your RV or we’ll tow it.”

The 3rd person nods in agreement. Probably a parking attendant or something.

Kate looks at Ariana who looks much more alive than earlier that afternoon and sighs, “Barely 3 days in the country and I already despise it here… Give us an hour and we’ll go someplace else. You can keep your stupid beach all to yourself…”

She pulls Ariana inside the camper smacking the door closed behind them.

Inside she hugs Ariana, “Hey, welcome back!” She whispers at her. “I see you found yourself? Feeling better?”

Ariana nods. “Bar… Eh, you’re a dumbo Kate. Always making trouble with everyone…” she accuses her.

Kate laughs. “Yea well, it’s not like they can do anything about it. We’re not from around here.”

“They can still arrest you, you know?” Ariana cautions her.

Kate Hmms and says, “You’re right. I’m sorry. So, what did you do? You look much better. Did you have a good time clearing your head?”

“I eh, did some thinking and I realized I can’t be without you. No matter how much of a bitch you are sometimes.” Ariana says with downcast eyes, expecting a reprimand for her words.

Instead Kate just laughs and ruffles her hair. “Damn straight, just as I can’t live without my silly wife either.”

She leans her forehead against Ariana’s and they grin at each other. As they so often do.

Someone slaps repeatedly on the door. “Hey! You two. Tow truck is on its way, if you’re not gone in 20 minutes we’ll remove you.”

Kate rolls her eyes. “Welcome to America…” She sighs. And through the door she shouts, “OK OK, I Said we’ll leave right? Geez.”

Ariana peeks outside a minute later but nobody is in sight. So she steps outside and folds up the awning and puts the starlink inside.

10 minutes later they’re ready to go and Kate drives off the lot ignoring the signals from the parking attendants and the police throwing her parking ticket out the window and joins the traffic on the coastal highway heading northbound.

“So uh, do you really think I’m a bitch?” Kate asks.

“Maybe…” Ariana evades the question.

Kate frowns but doesn’t say anything.
