"Ani here is our best rubber crafter. We brought some stuff in from other houses, but they made some personal bits for you. You can have a good old explore later. There's more," Jay said, pressing a button next to the armoire.

Just like Jane's, another compartment slid open and the sleeping pod was revealed.

"Just like mine," Jane said with a smile, gesturing toward the revealed compartment.

Alex stepped closer and peered inside. The sleeping pod was identical to Jane's, inner cage bars, soft padding, dim lighting. A cocoon. A safe place to surrender completely.

Her shoulders dropped, tension she hadn't realized she was carrying releasing all at once. Her own place to stay. Her own pod to sleep in, safe in the AI's care. Her own *space*.

A thought struck her suddenly, a flash of worry. Would she still get to sleep with Jane sometimes?

Jane must have read the expression on her face, because she immediately stepped closer, wrapping an arm around Alex's waist.

"You can still sleep in mine whenever you like," Jane said warmly. "But we want you to have a place of your own too. Our shifts won't always line up exactly, sometimes I'll be out on assignment and you'll need a little time to collect yourself, work on your own things too."

Alex looked at her and nodded, the worry easing. She could have both. Her own space *and* time with Jane. The best of both worlds.

"Some other little special bits," Jay said, and Alex turned to see him walking to the other corner of the room. He pressed something, and a panel on a tall rectangular wardrobe slid open, revealing a compartment inside.

"Since you were so keen on it at the market," Jay continued, his voice warm with amusement, "I thought you might like your own personal drone storage."

Alex's breath caught as she stared at the revealed space. It was just big enough for one drone to fit, standing upright. Armpit rests on either side padded with that quilted leather, designed to hold a drone suspended and immobilized, and another between the legs. The rest of the interior was like a cutout of a drone. Once inside with the panel closed, there would be no getting out and very little movement.

Her knees went weak. *Oh god.* The image flashed through her mind immediately, herself, suited and hooded, mounted on those bars, arms held in place, mask and suit stuck on, completely helpless. Stored away like equipment. Like a *thing*.

"When you need a little time to recharge, so to speak," Jay said, and even through the mask she could hear the smile in his voice.

Alex realized she was breathing hard, her heart racing. Jay must have been watching her biometrics, because he gave a soft chuckle.

"And this one was my specialty," Ani said, their short frame practically bouncing over to what looked like a heap of rubber with straps leading up from it to the ceiling. "Since you seem to have a particular thing for being all bagged up, this is your own little storage bag. Just climb in here," Ani said, stepping over the edges of the thick rubber bag. "Slap that button or ask the AI and up you go."

Ani smacked the button on the wall, and Alex watched, transfixed, as the motor hummed to life. The straps began reeling upward, pulling taut. Ani crouched down inside the thick rubber bag, sitting with their knees pulled up against their chest, arms wrapped around their legs.

The bag lifted up around them, the rubber cinching tight as it rose, forming a cocoon, no, a teardrop shape. The straps pulled the gathered rubber closed at the top, and then Ani was rising off the floor entirely, suspended in mid-air like a pendulum.

Ani was clearly having fun with it, wiggling and pushing against the rubber from the inside, making the whole thing sway and twist. Alex could swear she heard muffled giggles coming from inside the bag.

The sight hit Alex like a physical blow. Her knees went weak, and she grabbed onto Jane's arm for support, her breathing suddenly ragged.

*That could be me,* she thought, her mind spinning. *Sealed up, suspended, helpless, stored away.*

"Just lovely," Jay said, laughing, "we thought you'd like that." He tapped the side of his visor. "it doesn't take much to send you right into the deep arousal, does it?"

Alex flushed, suddenly aware that they could all see her biometrics. Could see exactly how turned on she was by the thought of being bagged and hung like… like cargo, anytime she wanted.

The winch reversed, lowering Ani back down to the floor. The motor slowed, and the bag settled onto the ground with the soft rustling of thick rubber. It loosened and draped down around Ani as they stood up, reaching up to push the opening wider and step out. They were still giggling, clearly delighted with themselves.

"I think it's fun," Ani said, brushing off their suit. "Might have to make one for myself."

"There are one or two other bits," Jay said, walking toward the far corner of the room and gesturing for Alex to follow. "A little desk for working, all made by yours truly."

Alex tore her eyes away from the suspension bag and looked where Jay was pointing. There was indeed a desk, simple, beautiful craftsmanship, made from reclaimed wood and painted matte black to match the armoire. A comfortable chair sat in front of it, and a small lamp on the corner.

"And a little something," Jay continued, "that the director of acquisitions picked up for you." He gestured to Ani with a flourish. "A little birdie said you like to play guitar."

Ani bounced over to a stand next to the desk, Alex hadn't even noticed it before, and carefully lifted the guitar from its stand. It was beautiful. Sleek and black with chrome hardware that gleamed in the soft light.

Ani carried it over and held it out to Alex.

"Oh my god," Alex breathed, taking it reverently. "It's…"

She threw the strap over her shoulder, the weight of it familiar and comforting. Her fingers found the frets automatically, muscle memory kicking in from years of practice. She strummed once, and the strings rang out clear and rich, if unamplified.

"Oh, this is *really* nice," she said, adjusting her grip and playing a quick riff. Her fingers moved over the strings, testing the action, the intonation. "Definitely nicer than my old one. I haven't played in…"

She trailed off, realizing she couldn't remember the last time she'd actually even seen her guitar.

"I'd ask how you know," Alex said, looking up at Ani, "but as always, not many secrets here."

Ani just grinned at her, that playful, bratty energy radiating even through their mask.

Ani reached out, and Alex carefully handed the guitar back to them, watching as they returned it to its stand.

"I picked out your initial wardrobe," Sam said, her voice quiet and gentle from back near the door. She'd been hanging back, letting Jay and Ani have their moments, but now she stepped forward slightly. "Got your bed all set, made sure you had your other basics. You'll have your own bin and basket in the bathroom now as well."

Her voice was so sweet, even filtered through the mask's amplification. Alex felt a rush of affection for her.

"If you ever need creature comforts," Sam continued, "you just come right to me. Okay?"

Alex looked at her and nodded, her throat suddenly tight. These people, these drones, her housemates, her *family* had spent days preparing this space for her. Making her feel welcome. Making her feel *home*.

"And any wardrobe changes you want, just ask me," Sam added. "I tried to pick things that I thought were on theme, but your self-expression in human mode is always up to you."

"That's all amazing," Alex said, and she could hear her voice wavering with emotion. "I'm sure it's great. Thank you." She looked around at all of them—Jay, Ani, Sam, Jane. "Thank all of you. For making me feel so at home here, for taking such good care of me."

Jane smiled at her, that warm, proud smile that made Alex's chest ache.

"That's what we do for any member of the collective," Jay said brightly. "And you're one of us now."

*One of us.* The words settled into Alex's bones. She wasn't a visitor anymore. She wasn't a guest or a trainee. She was one of them.

They could see that Alex was feeling a lot of emotions, hell, they could probably see her heart rate spiking on their HUDs, and they seemed to collectively decide to give her some space. They started moving toward the door, their movements coordinated in that way drones had when they were in sync.

"And with that," Jay said, "we'll let you have your peace and quiet. Have a good night, drone."

But Alex didn't let him leave just like that. She practically launched herself across the room and threw her arms around his midsection, hugging him tight.

"Thank you," she said against his chest, feeling the smooth rubber under her cheek.

"You're very welcome," Jay said, and she could hear the smile in his voice. His hand came up to hold her back gently.

When Alex finally released him, Ani was there, bouncing on their toes.

"Don't have too much fun with all this," Ani said, giving Alex's shoulder a playful punch. "Maybe sometime I could give them a try." They gestured back at the suspension bag with obvious enthusiasm.

Alex laughed, wiping at her eyes. "Anytime."

Then Sam. Alex held onto Sam for a long moment, just breathing, trying to convey everything she was feeling through the hug. Sam's arms came around her, gentle but firm, and they stood there together for several heartbeats.

Finally, Sam pulled away slowly, gave Alex a little nod through her mask, and walked down the hall after the others.

And then it was just the two of them. Alex and Jane.

The door was still open, and for a moment Alex was terrified that Jane would be next to leave. That she'd be left alone in this beautiful new room with all its equipment designed specifically for her darkest fantasies, with nothing but her own spiraling thoughts for company.

"Could…" Alex started, her voice small. "Could you stay with me?"

Jane's face softened immediately, all traces of playfulness replaced by pure affection.

"Of course, baby," she said, stepping forward and taking both of Alex's hands in hers. "I'm not going anywhere unless you want me to."

Alex sagged with relief. "I don't. Want you to go, I mean."

Jane smiled and turned to gently close the door, the soft *click* of the latch sealing them into Alex's new space together.
