"I didn't mean for it to happen now, it was just a thought that entered my head and I just went with it."

"Just went with it?" My tone was noticeably more angry.

Her voice became more pleading, "I'm sorry, Sam. I really didn't mean to get stuck. Please don't be too annoyed with me."

I glared at her as my hand came up and covered my mouth. Emily watched every small movement I made to determine my mood. I watched her lip quiver as she expected me to begin screaming at her and begin telling her off for doing such a stupid thing. However, it was my shoulders that were the first to give away how I really felt. They began rocking up and down before my eyes fluttered, stopping the gaze I was trying to hold.

"Sammy?" she sounded puzzled, trying to figure out what was happening.

A gasp left me, a sign that I was holding my breath for too long, and then the laughter started. It started as a small chuckle at the absurd moment, but seeing Emily look at me in that helpless state was enough to bring more out. My hand left my mouth as I doubled over, my lungs already hurt from laughing so much. As the noise echoed throughout the area, Emily just stood there, occasionally nervously joining me with a soft chuckle.

"I'm really sorry, Sam," Emily apologised again.

I tried to contain myself, managing to get the question out between moments, "What are you apologising for?"

"For… doing this in front of your mom. I didn't mean to be disrespectful."

"Disrespectful?" I asked, wiping a tear from my eye, "Mom would have found this hilarious!"

"She would?"

"Yeah! Mom wasn't a serious person at all, she loved situations like this."

The laughing continued for a while longer, each time I looked at my sister as she struggled with the device, it forced it to continue that little bit longer. Emily slowly realised that there was no angle with enough leverage that would unlock the cuffs, she would need help from another person to get them off.

"So… Can you help me?" Her voice was soft as she asked this.

My hands wiped my eyes again, removing the tears from them. This time they were not created by sadness, but a beautiful; yet bizarre moment, "Sure. Just give me a minute."

She might have been a goofball, she might have had her moments, but she had a pure heart. I also told my mother that she was right, there was such a thing as true love, I just didn't know it at the time she first told me.

The last mental sentence in my head was a promise. I promised her that I would come visit her again soon, Oxford was far away, but I will always stop by on the journey back. I rubbed my thumb along the top of the headstone, feeling the small imperfections on the edge before finally taking my hand away.

"Let's go" My smile widened as I approached her, placing my arm along her shoulder, turning her around and forcing her to begin walking down the same path we came up on.

"Okay. Yeah, the clasp is just there," Emily stated, moving her wrists closer to me and showing me the locked device, "It's just that thing there, just press it and I'll be free."

"Don't worry, Em. I know where it is."

"But… but you’re not pressing it."

"I love you so much," I giggled, placing my head against hers.

"I love you too, but… you're still not pressing it. You're going to let me out before we get to the car aren't you? Sammy?"

"We'll see."

Our shoes clicked along the moss covered stones. Large trees covered each side of the path, greeting us as we began the first steps towards the long journey to our next destination. The rising sun illuminated the path in front of us, the light bouncing off the occasional flower that was growing between the stones. I held my sister close. Despite all the unknown that was coming our way, it was a comfort that we would face it together, as sister's, as lovers and as a family.

"…Sammy?"
